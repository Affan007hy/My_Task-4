  1. Early Life and Education: He was born on November 11, 1888, in Mecca (now in Saudi Arabia) and later moved to Calcutta (now Kolkata), India. His original name was Abul Kalam Ghulam Muhiyuddin. He received a traditional Islamic education and was well-versed in Arabic, Persian, and Urdu languages.

  2. Involvement in the Independence Movement: Azad was deeply involved in the Indian independence movement from a young age. He became a leader of the Indian National Congress and played a key role in advocating for India's freedom from British colonial rule. He was known for his fiery speeches and writings that inspired many Indians to join the struggle for independence.

  3. Role in Congress: Azad was elected as the President of the Indian National Congress in 1923 and again in 1940. He worked alongside other leaders like Mahatma Gandhi, Jawaharlal Nehru, and Sardar Patel to coordinate the movement against the British rule.

  4. Promotion of Unity: Maulana Azad was a staunch supporter of Hindu-Muslim unity and worked towards fostering harmony among different religious and cultural communities in India. He was committed to the idea of a united India where people of all religions coexist peacefully.

  5. Education and Literacy: Azad was a strong advocate for education and literacy. He believed that education was crucial for the development and progress of India. He played a significant role in establishing educational institutions like the Jamia Millia Islamia University in Delhi.

  6. Post-Independence Contributions: After India gained independence in 1947, Azad became the first Minister of Education in independent India. He played a crucial role in shaping the education policies of the newly-formed nation.

  7. Death and Legacy: Maulana Azad passed away on February 22, 1958. He is remembered as one of the leading figures of the Indian freedom movement and a symbol of communal harmony and secularism. His contributions to education, unity, and social justice continue to inspire generations in India.

Maulana Azad's life and work remain an important part of India's history, and he is celebrated for his dedication to the cause of freedom and his efforts to build a united and inclusive India.
